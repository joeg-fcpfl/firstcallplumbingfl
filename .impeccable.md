# First Call Plumbing — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Two overlapping audiences, both doing the same job: **quick credibility vetting before picking up the phone**.

- **General contractors** — sub-hunting, need to confirm licensing, service area, and scope fast
- **Building owners / property managers** — less technical, need to feel confident they're calling a legitimate, experienced operation

Both are likely on a phone or laptop during a workday. Both want to be done in under a minute. Neither is browsing for fun.

### Brand Personality
**No-bullshit. Reliable. South Florida local.**

- No-bullshit: Direct, confident, nothing decorative that doesn't earn its place
- Reliable: Grounded and solid — not trendy, not startup-y, not flashy tech
- South Florida local: Warmth, sun, heat — not generic "commercial plumbing blue"

Emotional goal: the visitor should feel like they're calling a company that has been around, knows the counties, and won't waste their time.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Light theme.** This is a daytime business tool, browsed in offices, job sites, and bright Florida light — not a nighttime product. The current dark navy + glassmorphism reads as tech SaaS, not South Florida trades.

**Direction: Industrial Print + South Florida Sun**
- Think: blueprint title blocks, painted work van lettering, embossed metal signage, sun-faded stucco walls
- NOT: startup landing page, AI SaaS aesthetic, generic "corporate blue" contractor site, tropical clichés

**Color palette** (OKLCH-based):
- Background: warm off-white (slightly amber-tinted, not pure white)
- Text: deep charcoal with a warm cast (not pure black, not navy-blue-tinted)
- Primary accent: warm amber/burnt orange — South Florida sun, heat, urgency
- Secondary: slate blue — Florida water, plumbing/pipe associations, credibility
- Neutrals tinted toward amber, not blue

**Typography:**
- Display: **Big Shoulders Display** — industrial, condensed, no-bullshit. Reads like signage or a blueprint header, not a SaaS product.
- Body: **Hanken Grotesk** — clean, readable, professional without being generic
- (Both are Google Fonts, neither is on the reflex list)

### Design Principles
1. **Directness over decoration** — Every element justifies its existence. No glassmorphism, no grid overlays, no blur effects for atmosphere.
2. **Built-in confidence** — The design should feel like a company that's been doing this for decades. Solid, grounded, not chasing trends.
3. **South Florida sun, not South Florida kitsch** — Warm, bright, high-contrast. No palm trees, no tropical patterns.
4. **Hierarchy serves the vetting job** — License number, phone number, service counties, and service scope should be immediately visible. The visitor should be able to confirm credibility in under 10 seconds.
5. **Typography carries the weight** — Strong condensed display type for impact, clean body for readability. Motion is minimal and purposeful.
