The One Critical Limitation

The contact form does not actually send you anything on a free static host. Right now the form shows a "Message Received" confirmation when submitted — but no email is ever sent and no data is stored anywhere. A potential client who fills out the form thinking they've contacted you will never hear back, because you never received their message.

This is fixable without building a backend. A free add-on service called Web3Forms (or similar) plugs directly into the existing form with about 10 lines of code. It emails you every submission with all the fields — name, company, phone, service type, project details — the moment someone hits Submit.

Other Limitations on the Free Static Route

  • ✗ No appointment scheduling — clients can't book an estimate online without a separate tool
  • ✗ No content updates without a developer — changing a service description or adding a photo requires editing code and redeploying
  • ✗ No lead tracking — no way to see all your inquiries in one place or track quote status
  • ✗ No automated follow-ups — no email sequences to nurture PM contract leads
  • ✗ No customer portal — clients can't check job status or invoice history online

Bottom line on free hosting: For a business where clients primarily call you, the free route gets a professional site live immediately. Add Web3Forms and the contact form works. You're not leaving money on the table — you're just not capturing every lead that prefers to fill out a form over calling.

What These Features Mean in Practice

Real contact form emails — Every inquiry lands in your inbox immediately with name, company, phone, email, service type, county, and project details. An auto-reply also goes to the customer confirming you received their message.

Estimate self-scheduling — Property managers and GCs can pick a date and time from your available slots without playing phone tag. Reminder emails go out automatically 24 hours before. You control available times from a simple admin panel.

PM contract lead funnel — A dedicated page for preventive maintenance contracts with an automated email sequence: Day 1 thank you, Day 3 case study, Day 7 call-to-action. Turns a form submission into a warmed-up lead.

Admin dashboard — See all inquiries in one place. Mark each one as "contacted," "quoted," "won," or "lost." Track which services drive the most leads. Export to a spreadsheet anytime.
